LPM ships the development infrastructure most projects bolt together from five separate tools — tunneling, local HTTPS, secrets, port reservations, dependency-graph visualization, and project health checks — as first-class commands. Everything in this section assumes [`lpm dev`](/docs/dev) is your default; these commands are what `lpm dev` orchestrates under the hood and what you reach for when you need to inspect, configure, or fix something it touched.

<Cards>
  <Card title="lpm tunnel" href="/docs/infra/tunnel" description="Expose localhost to the internet. Claim a stable domain, capture and replay webhooks." />
  <Card title="lpm cert" href="/docs/infra/cert" description="Local HTTPS — generate certificates, install the LPM CA into your trust store, inspect status." />
  <Card title="lpm ports" href="/docs/infra/ports" description="List, kill, or reset dev-service port assignments." />
  <Card title="lpm graph" href="/docs/infra/graph" description="Render the dependency graph as tree, DOT, Mermaid, JSON, stats, or HTML." />
  <Card title="lpm doctor" href="/docs/infra/doctor" description="Project + environment health check. Auto-fix common issues." />
  <Card title="lpm store" href="/docs/infra/store" description="Verify, list, or garbage-collect the global content-addressable store." />
</Cards>
